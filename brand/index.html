01

The mark

A pixel-art Z monogram on a 12×12 grid — a bold Z inside a capsule of top and bottom staples and continuous side bars, with two open windows. It is deliberately blocky: render crisp-edged, never anti-aliased, smoothed, or redrawn as a rounded vector. Monochrome by design — white on dark and warm grounds, near-black on light; the signature treatment is white on the espresso field.

03

Typography

Mono-forward — zkCoins speaks in hashes, addresses, and proofs, and the wordmark is set in monospace. IBM Plex Mono carries the identity (wordmark, labels, data); IBM Plex Sans carries running copy. Both are SIL OFL, so shipping the webfonts is license-clean; surfaces fall back to each platform's default faces until the fonts are loaded.

04

Voice & messaging

Precise, calm, and honest. We explain privacy in plain language for people who aren't cryptographers, and stay technically exact for those who are. We never overpromise, never say “moon,” and never claim more privacy than the protocol delivers.

The name — always exactly this

CorrectzkCoins
WrongZKCoins · Zkcoins · zk coins · ZK Coins

Lowercase zk, capital C, one word. Lowercase zkcoins only in domains and handles (zkcoins.com, @zkcoins).

Tagline & one-liner

Private Bitcoin transactions via Shielded CSV
zkCoins is a shielded pool on Bitcoin. Send and receive without exposing amounts or who paid whom — secured by zero-knowledge proofs, settled in real BTC.

Say this, not that

Don't — hype / jargon
The next-gen privacy revolution. Untraceable coins, guaranteed anonymity.
Do — precise / plain
A shielded pool that hides amounts and links between payments. Privacy depends on the anonymity set.
Don't — wrong model
zkCoins is a new coin / a sidechain / a bridge to another chain.
Do — accurate framing
zkCoins is Bitcoin. Shielded CSV keeps funds in BTC — no new token, no bridge.
Don't — vague benefit
Bank-grade security and total peace of mind.
Do — verifiable claim
Every transaction carries a zero-knowledge proof anyone can verify.
